# AI-Powered SEO Content Production

Professional research repository on how leading SEO and growth practitioners are adapting content strategy, production, measurement, and distribution for AI-mediated search.

## Project Overview

This repository documents a practitioner-led research project on AI-powered SEO content production. The goal is to understand how experienced operators are responding to AI Search, AI Overviews, ChatGPT-style discovery, answer engines, and LLM citation behavior.

The work is intentionally focused on implementation rather than generic commentary. It collects expert materials, transcripts, LinkedIn analysis, source notes, and synthesis documents that can be used to build a practical point of view on modern SEO and content operations.

## Research Objective

The central research question:

> How should B2B SaaS teams adapt SEO content production for a search environment shaped by AI retrieval, synthesized answers, brand citations, and off-site source trust?

The research focuses on:

- How AI Search changes visibility, measurement, and content strategy
- How practitioners define GEO, AEO, LLMO, Relevance Engineering, and AI visibility
- Which content formats and channels influence AI citations
- How owned content, earned media, social platforms, reviews, videos, and communities work together
- What workflows marketers can use to produce content that is useful to humans and retrievable by AI systems

## Expert Selection Methodology

The expert set prioritizes operators with demonstrated implementation experience rather than broad AI influencers.

Selection criteria:

- Founder, operator, consultant, agency leader, SEO practitioner, or growth leader
- Demonstrated real-world SEO or content production experience
- Active publishing on LinkedIn, YouTube, blogs, newsletters, podcasts, or webinars
- Clear relevance to AI Search, content operations, GEO/AEO, LLM visibility, or AI visibility measurement
- Evidence of frameworks, case studies, research, tooling, or client-facing implementation

## Current Findings

### 1. AI Search Is A Relevance And Trust Problem

AI Search changes the unit of optimization from pages and rankings to prompts, passages, sources, citations, entities, and synthesized answers.

### 2. Measurement Needs To Move Beyond Rankings And Traffic

The research points toward a measurement model that includes AI visibility, citation visibility, share of voice, sentiment, source coverage, brand discoverability, referral quality, and pipeline influence.

### 3. Off-Site Reputation Is Becoming A Core SEO Asset

Review platforms, analyst pages, comparison content, communities, media mentions, social proof, YouTube, and LinkedIn can influence whether a brand is cited or recommended by AI systems.

### 4. Content Production Must Become A System

The strongest operators are not recommending mass AI article production. They point toward source mapping, prompt testing, candidate passages, technical accessibility, expert review, distribution, refresh loops, and measurement.

### 5. Human Expertise Moves Upstream

AI can accelerate research and drafting, but durable advantage still comes from strategy, audience understanding, original insight, editorial judgment, cross-channel coordination, and measurement design.

## Working Thesis

AI-powered SEO content production is not simply using AI to write more articles. The stronger opportunity is building a content and authority system that helps AI engines understand, trust, retrieve, cite, and recommend a brand across the buyer journey.

For B2B SaaS teams, that means combining classic SEO fundamentals with content engineering, off-site reputation building, AI visibility measurement, distribution, and human-led strategic judgment.
