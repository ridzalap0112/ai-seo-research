## 1. Off-Property Reputation Drives AI Citations

**Activity Type:** Shared by Kevin Indig  
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f  
**Date:** 2026-06-18  

**Summary:**  
Kevin argues that two brands can perform the same on-page optimization and still be cited by AI at different rates because the real gap is off-property reputation. AI systems reuse trust from the sources they retrieve from and favor entities they already recognize for a topic.

**Key Insight:**  
Owned content is only one AI citation input, and often a weaker one. Third-party mentions from publications, analysts, competitors, review sites, and other trusted sources can decide whether AI systems trust the brand.

**Why It Matters:**  
AI-powered SEO content production cannot stop at blog optimization. Teams need a source-ecosystem strategy that earns mentions in the places AI systems already cite.

## 4. AI Overviews Change Search Behavior

**Activity Type:** Liked by Kevin Indig  
**Post URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/vanbuskirk_when-an-ai-overview-is-present-the-median-activity-7473013848036233216-phRy  
**Date:** 2026-06-17  

**Summary:**  
The post highlights user-behavior research showing that when an AI Overview appears, users spend substantially more time scrolling backward up the page compared with searches without an AI Overview.

**Key Insight:**  
AI Overviews alter how users consume SERPs. They can cause more pausing, reconsideration, and reverse scrolling before a click.

**Why It Matters:**  
Content strategy and SEO reporting need to account for changed user behavior on AI-enhanced SERPs. Visibility can matter even when click paths become less direct.
