## Expert Background

Ross Hudgens is the founder and CEO of Siege Media, a content marketing and SEO agency known for data-led content, digital PR, and scalable organic growth programs. His perspective is valuable because it comes from agency-side implementation across multiple business models rather than individual creator commentary.

## Core SEO Philosophy

Ross tends to frame SEO as a business-growth function, not a collection of page-level tactics. His work emphasizes content formats that can earn links, rank, and contribute to measurable growth. In the AI-search era, that philosophy extends to understanding which content types are likely to influence AI assistants and which are unlikely to produce meaningful LLM traffic.

## AI Search Perspective

Ross treats AI search as a channel that needs executive translation and measurement discipline. His recent content suggests that AI search impact varies by business model: B2B content may be better positioned for LLM-assisted research journeys than broad B2C editorial content.

The strongest research angle is segmentation. Instead of asking whether AI search is good or bad for content marketing, Ross's work points toward asking which queries, content types, industries, and funnel stages are actually receiving AI-driven visibility or referral traffic.

## Content Production Frameworks

- Segment content by business model before estimating AI-search upside.
- Prioritize bottom-funnel and decision-support content where AI tools are likely to assist research.
- Connect content production with digital PR and brand authority building.
- Measure LLM referrals separately from classic organic sessions.
- Translate AI search opportunities into language that CMOs and executives can act on.
